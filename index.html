What is Dengue Fever?

Dengue fever is a mosquito-borne viral illness caused by dengue virus (DENV-1 to DENV-4). It spreads mainly through bites from infected Aedes aegypti and Aedes albopictus mosquitoes. Over 3.9 billion people are at risk worldwide, with outbreaks in more than 100 countries.

Symptoms

Mild Dengue

  • High fever, severe headache
  • Pain behind eyes, joint and muscle pain
  • Nausea, vomiting, fatigue
  • Skin rash, mild bleeding

Severe Dengue

  • Severe abdominal pain
  • Persistent vomiting
  • Severe bleeding (nose, gums, internal)
  • Blood in stool/vomit, shock

Prevention

  • Use mosquito repellent
  • Wear protective clothing
  • Sleep under mosquito nets
  • Eliminate stagnant water
  • Community fogging during outbreaks

Treatment

No specific antiviral cure exists. Treatment is supportive:

  • Rest and fluids (ORS, juices, soups)
  • Paracetamol for fever
