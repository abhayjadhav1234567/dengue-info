What is Dengue Fever?

Dengue fever is a mosquito-borne viral infection caused by four types of dengue virus (DENV-1 to DENV-4). It spreads through the bite of infected Aedes aegypti and Aedes albopictus mosquitoes. Symptoms can range from mild fever to severe, life-threatening dengue hemorrhagic fever. More than 3.9 billion people across 120+ countries are at risk.

Symptoms

High Fever

Sudden high fever up to 104°F (40°C).

Severe Headache

Intense headache & pain behind the eyes.

Body Pain

Muscle, joint, and bone pain (“breakbone fever”).

Bleeding

Nose/gum bleeding, easy bruising, rashes.

Prevention

Avoid Mosquito Bites

Use repellents, nets, and protective clothing.

Remove Stagnant Water

Clean containers, drains & open water tanks.

Community Fogging

Neighborhood spraying reduces outbreaks.

Treatment

Currently, there is no specific antiviral treatment for dengue. Care is supportive and focuses on relieving symptoms and preventing complications:

  • 💧 Drink plenty of fluids (ORS, soups, juices).
  • 💊 Use paracetamol for fever & pain (avoid aspirin & ibuprofen).
  • 🏥 Hospitalization for severe cases: IV fluids, oxygen, blood transfusions if needed.
  • ⚠️ Monitor warning signs: persistent vomiting, bleeding, abdominal pain, dizziness.
