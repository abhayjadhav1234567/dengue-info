What is Dengue Fever?

Dengue fever is a mosquito-borne viral infection caused by **DENV-1 to DENV-4**. It spreads through the bite of infected *Aedes aegypti* and *Aedes albopictus* mosquitoes. About **390 million infections occur every year** across more than 120 countries.

Symptoms

High Fever

Sudden fever up to 104°F.

Severe Headache

Intense headache & pain behind the eyes.

Body Pain

Muscle, joint, and bone pain (“breakbone fever”).

Bleeding

Nose/gum bleeding, skin rashes.

Prevention

Avoid Bites

Use repellents, nets, and long-sleeved clothes.

Remove Stagnant Water

Prevent mosquito breeding sites.

Fogging

Community fogging reduces outbreaks.

Treatment

  • 💧 Stay hydrated: water, ORS, juices, soups.
  • 💊 Use paracetamol for fever (avoid aspirin/ibuprofen).
  • 🏥 Severe cases: IV fluids, blood transfusion if required.
  • ⚠️ Watch for warning signs: vomiting, bleeding, abdominal pain.

Dengue Fever – Effect on Human Organs

Organ Normal Work What Dengue Virus Does What Problem Happens
Liver 🟠 Cleans blood, makes proteins, helps digestion Virus enters liver cells → immune attack → swelling Poor cleaning, less clotting → bleeding risk, liver failure
Bone Marrow 🔴 Produces RBC, WBC, platelets Virus slows marrow → fewer platelets Low platelets → gum bleeding, nosebleeds, internal bleeding
Blood Vessels 🔵 Carry blood safely without leakage Walls become weak & leaky Blood pressure drops → organ damage → shock
Spleen 🟣 Filters blood, supports immunity Overworks and swells up Pain in left belly, rare rupture → internal bleeding
Brain 🟡 (rare) Controls body functions Swelling or bleeding in brain tissue Headache, confusion, seizures, unconsciousness
Heart 💚 (rare) Pumps blood to whole body Virus weakens heart muscles Weak pumping → fatigue, chest pain, breathlessness
